# Bright Smile Dental — Emergency Protocol

## CRITICAL: Call 911 or Go to the Nearest Emergency Room

The following situations require immediate emergency medical attention. **Do not come to the dental office first — call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room immediately:**

- Uncontrolled bleeding that does not stop with firm pressure after 15 minutes
- Difficulty breathing or swallowing
- Facial or neck swelling that is compromising the airway
- Suspected jaw fracture (inability to move the jaw, severe misalignment after trauma)
- Severe facial trauma with suspected facial bone fracture or loss of consciousness
- **Dental abscess with swelling spreading to the neck, floor of mouth, or under the jaw, especially if accompanied by difficulty swallowing or high fever** — this may indicate Ludwig's angina, a life-threatening infection that can close the airway within hours. Call 911 immediately.

## Dental Emergencies — Same-Day Care

Call our office at **(555) 123-4567** for same-day emergency appointments for:

- **Cracked or broken tooth** — save any pieces, rinse mouth with warm water.
- **Severe toothache** — persistent, throbbing pain that disrupts sleep or daily activities.
- **Knocked-out (avulsed) permanent tooth** — this is TIME-CRITICAL. Handle the tooth by the crown (not the root), rinse gently without scrubbing, keep the tooth moist in milk or saliva. You need to be seen ideally within 30 minutes, and no later than 60 minutes, for the best chance of re-implantation. **If our earliest appointment is more than 1 hour away, go to the nearest emergency dentist immediately.** Note: Do not attempt to reinsert a baby (primary/milk) tooth — contact us and we will advise you.
- **Abscess or facial swelling** without spreading to the neck/floor of mouth — may indicate infection requiring urgent treatment. If swelling is spreading to the neck or you develop a high fever, call 911 (see above).

## Red Flags — Urgent Care (Same-Day or Next-Day)

Contact us promptly if you experience:

- Spreading swelling accompanied by fever — possible sign of spreading infection. If swelling involves the neck or floor of mouth, call 911 instead.
- Persistent numbness after a dental procedure that does not resolve within the expected timeframe.
- Inability to open the mouth (trismus) — may indicate infection or TMJ issue.

## Prompt Care (Within 1-3 Days)

The following are not emergencies but should be addressed soon:

- **Lost filling** — keep the area clean and avoid chewing on that side.
- **Lost crown** — if you have the crown and it seats easily, try placing it back with denture adhesive as a temporary measure. Do not force the crown if it does not seat easily. Do not attempt this if you believe you may have swallowed or inhaled the crown — seek medical attention. Call us to schedule a recement appointment within 1-3 days.

## First Aid Tips While You Wait

### Knocked-Out Permanent Tooth
1. Pick up the tooth by the crown (the white part you can see in the mouth). Do not touch the root.
2. Rinse gently with water if dirty — do not scrub or remove tissue fragments.
3. If possible, place the tooth back in the socket and bite down gently on a clean cloth to hold it in place.
4. If you cannot reinsert it, place the tooth in a container of cold milk or your own saliva. Do not let the tooth dry out.
5. Get to a dentist ideally within 30 minutes, and no later than 60 minutes, for the best outcome. If our office cannot see you within that window, go to the nearest emergency dentist.

**Important:** Do not attempt to replant a baby (primary/milk) tooth — this can damage the developing permanent tooth underneath. Contact us and we will advise you.

### Severe Tooth Pain
- If not contraindicated, over-the-counter ibuprofen (400 mg for adults) may help — check with your pharmacist if you are unsure about drug interactions or allergies.
- Apply a cold compress to the outside of the cheek: 20 minutes on, 20 minutes off.
- Avoid hot, cold, or sweet foods and drinks.
- Do not place aspirin directly on the gums — this can cause a chemical burn.

### Broken or Chipped Tooth
- Rinse your mouth with warm salt water.
- If there is bleeding, apply gentle pressure with gauze.
- Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling.
- Save any tooth fragments and bring them to your appointment.
