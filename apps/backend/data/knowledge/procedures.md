# Bright Smile Dental — Procedures & Services

## Preventive Care

### Cleaning (Prophylaxis)
- **Duration:** approximately 30 minutes
- Routine cleaning to remove plaque and tartar buildup. Recommended every 6 months.

### Deep Cleaning / Scaling & Root Planing
- **Duration:** approximately 60-90 minutes
- Treatment for gum disease (periodontal disease). Removes bacteria and tartar below the gumline. May require local anesthesia.
- **After your deep cleaning:** Some sensitivity, soreness, and minor gum tenderness are normal for 3-7 days (longer than a routine cleaning). Rinse with warm salt water, eat soft foods for the first day, and avoid vigorous brushing near the treated areas. A follow-up periodontal maintenance visit will be scheduled to check healing.

### Fluoride Treatments
- Professional fluoride application to strengthen tooth enamel and prevent cavities.
- Especially recommended for children and adults at higher risk for cavities.

### Dental Sealants
- Thin protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth (molars) to prevent cavities.
- Most commonly recommended for children and teens, but adults with cavity-prone molars may benefit as well.

## Diagnostic

### Checkup / Comprehensive Exam
- **Duration:** approximately 30 minutes
- Includes oral examination, digital X-rays if needed, oral cancer screening, and treatment plan discussion.

### Consultation
- **Duration:** approximately 20 minutes
- Meet with the dentist to discuss specific concerns, review treatment options, or get a second opinion.

## Restorative

### Filling
- **Duration:** approximately 30-60 minutes
- Tooth-colored composite fillings to repair cavities. Duration depends on the number and size of fillings.

### Crown
- **Duration:** 2 visits, approximately 60 minutes each
- Visit 1: Tooth preparation, impression, and temporary crown placement.
- Visit 2: Permanent crown fitting and cementation.

### Root Canal
- **Duration:** approximately 60-90 minutes
- Removes infected pulp tissue from inside the tooth, followed by sealing. A crown is strongly recommended afterward, especially for back teeth (molars and premolars), to protect against fracture since the tooth becomes more brittle after treatment.

### Extraction
- **Duration:** approximately 30-60 minutes
- Removal of a tooth that cannot be saved. Includes simple and surgical extractions.
- **After your extraction:** Bite on gauze for 30-45 minutes to control bleeding. For the first 24-48 hours: avoid using straws, smoking, spitting forcefully, or rinsing vigorously (these can dislodge the blood clot and cause dry socket). Eat soft foods and take pain relief as directed.
- **Watch for dry socket:** If pain worsens significantly 3-5 days after extraction (rather than improving), you may have dry socket — call us right away. Other red flags: foul taste, visible bone in the socket, or pain radiating to the ear.

## Cosmetic

### Teeth Whitening
- **Duration:** approximately 60 minutes (in-office)
- Professional-grade whitening for noticeably brighter results in a single visit.

### Veneers
- **Duration:** 2 visits required
- Custom porcelain shells bonded to the front of teeth for a transformed smile.
- Veneers require removal of a thin layer of enamel and are not reversible. Your dentist will assess whether veneers are appropriate for your teeth during a consultation.

## Implants & Prosthetics

### Dental Implants
- Multi-visit procedure offered at our office.
- Titanium post placed in the jawbone to replace a missing tooth root, topped with a custom crown.
- Some patients may need a bone graft prior to implant placement if bone volume is insufficient — this is evaluated at the consultation and may add an additional healing period before the implant can be placed.

### Night Guards (Bruxism / TMJ)
- Custom-fitted mouth guard to protect teeth from grinding during sleep and relieve jaw tension.
- Requires an impressions appointment followed by a fitting appointment.
- May also be recommended for TMJ (temporomandibular joint) issues — see your dentist for evaluation.

## Sedation & Comfort Options

We offer the following comfort options for patients with dental anxiety or for longer procedures:

- **Nitrous oxide ("laughing gas")** — mild sedation you breathe through a mask; wears off quickly after the procedure.
- **Oral sedation** — a prescribed medication taken before your appointment for moderate relaxation. You will need someone to drive you to and from your appointment.
- **Comfort amenities** — noise-canceling headphones, TV during procedures, warm blankets, and a gentle patient-paced approach.

Discuss sedation options with your dentist during your consultation or let us know when scheduling.

## Referral-Only Services

The following services require a referral to a specialist:

- **Dentures and partials** — referred to a prosthodontist.
- **Orthodontics** — referred to an orthodontist, except for Dr. Michael Chen's Tuesday consultation hours at our office.

*Note: All procedure durations are approximate and may vary based on individual patient needs.*
